## Key Business Findings
### 1. Morning Rush Drives Maximum Revenue

The analysis shows that revenue and transaction volume are strongly concentrated during the morning period. The highest revenue occurs between 8 AM and 10 AM, with 10 AM generating the maximum revenue.

This indicates that customer demand is closely linked to morning routines such as commuting, breakfast purchases, and workday preparation.

**Business Recommendation:**

Afficionado Coffee Roasters should schedule maximum staff between 8 AM and 11 AM. Inventory for high-demand products such as coffee, tea, and bakery items should be replenished before the morning rush begins.

### 2. Revenue Distribution is Balanced Across Stores

Revenue contribution is distributed relatively evenly across Hell's Kitchen, Astoria, and Lower Manhattan. Each location contributes approximately one-third of total revenue.

This indicates that the business is not overly dependent on a single store location, reducing operational risk and improving revenue stability.

**Business Recommendation:**

Maintain balanced inventory allocation and staffing levels across all locations. Store-specific strategies can still be implemented, but no location currently requires major corrective intervention.

### 3. Coffee and Tea Drive the Majority of Revenue

Coffee and Tea are the highest-performing product categories across all stores. These categories consistently generate the largest share of revenue throughout the day.

The strong performance of these categories highlights their importance in customer purchasing behavior and overall business profitability.

**Business Recommendation:**

Prioritize inventory planning, promotional campaigns, and product innovation around Coffee and Tea offerings. Seasonal variations and premium options may further increase revenue potential.

### 4. Evening Hours Present Revenue Growth Opportunities

Revenue declines significantly after the morning peak period. Evening and late-hour demand remains substantially lower than morning demand across all locations.

This indicates that customer engagement is concentrated during traditional coffee consumption periods, leaving untapped revenue opportunities later in the day.

**Business Recommendation:**

Introduce evening-specific promotions such as bakery bundles, discounted beverages, loyalty rewards, and limited-time offers to encourage customer traffic during low-demand periods.

### 5. Demand-Based Staffing Can Improve Operational Efficiency

Hourly transaction analysis reveals clear differences between peak and low-demand periods. Morning hours require significantly higher staffing levels compared to evening hours.

Aligning workforce allocation with actual customer demand can reduce labor inefficiencies while maintaining service quality.

**Business Recommendation:**

Implement demand-based staffing schedules with maximum workforce allocation between 8 AM and 11 AM, standard staffing during afternoon hours, and reduced staffing during low-demand evening periods.

## Conclusion

The analysis demonstrates that Afficionado Coffee Roasters operates in a demand environment strongly influenced by time-of-day purchasing behavior. Morning hours drive the majority of revenue, while Coffee and Tea remain the primary revenue-generating categories across all store locations.

The findings support data-driven decision making in staffing, inventory planning, product strategy, and promotional activities. By aligning operational resources with observed demand patterns, the organization can improve efficiency, customer experience, and overall profitability.
